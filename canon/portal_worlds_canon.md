# Portal Worlds Canon

## Purpose

Portal worlds are the main early and mid campaign focus of The Central Nexus.

They are not small side activities.

Each portal world can function as its own D&D 5e campaign, potentially from level 1 to level 20.

## Core Rules

When a player enters a portal world, they create or activate a character specific to that world.

The player's Nexus identity persists, but the portal-world character is mechanically distinct.

A player can have multiple portal-world characters across different worlds.

A portal-world character can return to the Central Nexus.

A portal-world character may eventually attempt the Spire/Tower.

## World-Specific Boundaries

Items from one portal world cannot be used in another portal world.

Followers from one portal world cannot be used in another portal world.

World-specific rewards may be usable in the Central Nexus and Spire/Tower only if explicitly export-approved.

This prevents balance collapse and preserves each world's identity.

## Followers

Followers are earned through quest lines and are the same level as the portal-world player character.

Followers should have full story treatment:

- name
- role
- backstory
- current situation
- personal quest
- relationship progression
- combat/support function
- export limits
- Nexus availability
- Tower availability
- injury/death/retirement consequences
