# Canon Summary — The Central Nexus: The Tower of Ascension

## Core Premise

The Central Nexus is an MMO-style social/faction hub where player identities, avatar forms, faction standing, Nexus Credits, portal access, and Tower/Spire access are managed.

The Central Nexus is not just a prologue city. It is the persistent hub layer of the campaign.

The portal worlds are the main early campaign focus. They are not minor side quests. Each portal world can support its own D&D 5e campaign path from level 1 to level 20.

The Spire/Tower is the lethal ascent challenge. It is not the forgiving early tutorial dungeon.

## Account / Nexus Identity

Each player has a persistent Nexus identity. This is the account/soul/social identity that exists in the Central Nexus.

The Nexus identity tracks:

- three lives
- Nexus reputation
- faction standing
- Nexus Credits
- portal-world unlocks
- Tower/Spire access status
- account-wide relationships
- account-wide secrets and clues
- world-character roster
- follower roster
- public records
- legal status
- debts
- heat/criminal attention

## Portal-World Characters

When a player enters a portal world, they create or activate a character specifically for that world.

That portal-world character:

- uses D&D 5e mechanics
- has its own class/species/background/build
- has its own level
- has its own gear
- has its own followers
- has its own quest progress
- has its own local reputation
- may return to the Central Nexus
- may eventually attempt the Spire/Tower

## Item and Follower Boundaries

Items and followers from one portal world cannot be used in another portal world.

This is a balance rule and a world-identity rule.

- A pirate-world relic should not break a magic academy campaign.
- A martial-cosmos follower should not solve a cyber-noir mystery unless a special cross-world exception is explicitly unlocked.

World-specific gear and followers may appear in:

- their home portal world
- the Central Nexus, if unlocked and allowed
- the Spire/Tower, if the character is eligible and the item/follower is export-approved

Nexus-native and Tower-native rewards are separate categories.

## Followers

Followers are earned through quest lines.

Followers must not be shallow stat sticks. A follower should have:

- name
- ancestry/species
- class or role
- level
- full backstory
- current motive
- personal quest line
- relationship to the player character
- relationship to the portal world
- reason they can or cannot enter the Nexus
- reason they can or cannot join Tower attempts
- death/injury/retirement consequences

Followers are the same level as the player character from their portal world.

Followers should support play, not replace PCs.

## Portal Worlds

Known portal-world concepts:

- **Alpha** — magic academy / occult academy
- **Beta** — martial cosmos / combat discipline world
- **Gamma** — shattered seas / pirate world
- **Delta** — neon metropolis / cyber-noir
- **Epsilon** — fantasy online / game-world meta
- **Zeta** — heist city / thieves, contracts, and underworld intrigue
- **Eta** — red frontier / survival western
- **Theta** — ashen kingdoms / dark fantasy war

Each portal world needs:

- public name
- portal name
- genre
- character creation rules
- local campaign premise
- local factions
- local map/gazetteer
- major NPCs
- major villains
- follower quest lines
- reward categories
- export/import rules
- Tower-preparation value
- full 1–20 campaign arc

## True Spire Floor 1

The true Spire Floor 1 is The Executioner.

The Executioner is not a level-2 tutorial boss.

The Executioner is a lethal CR 10 extreme-threat guardian. Low-level parties should be expected to die, retreat, extract, or learn the lesson that the Spire is not forgiving.

The player-facing terminal says:

- Danger Level: Extreme
- Lives lost gathering intelligence: 47
- Success rate: 23%
- Threat designation: The Executioner
- Party size recommended: 4–6
- Emergency extraction plan mandatory
- Backup lives recommended
- Magical weapons preferred
- Freedom of movement recommended
- Burst healing recommended
- Lightning protection recommended
- Type ACKNOWLEDGE RISK to proceed
- Type RETURN to exit
- In the Spire, death has meaning

## The Executioner

The Executioner is:

- Large fiend/demon
- chaotic evil
- AC 17
- HP 185
- CR 10
- has Death Sentence Protocol
- suppresses healing
- worsens death saves
- has magic resistance
- has legendary resistance
- grapples isolated targets
- executes grappled targets
- uses Chains of Judgment
- has legendary actions
- has lair actions
- fights in a 120-foot circular arena
- has lightning hazards
- has shifting terrain
- manifests spectral witnesses

## Canon Repair Requirement

Any existing manuscript material that treats Floors 1–3 as safe level-2 progression is draft material and must be repaired.

The current "Threshold That Weighs You," "Shortcut That Smiles," and "Listening Dark" material may be preserved only as:

- Spire Seeker Orientation Trials
- Forecourt training simulations
- nonlethal prep gauntlets
- optional training appendix material

They are not actual Spire Floors 1–3.
They do not grant Tower floor clears.
They do not bypass The Executioner.
